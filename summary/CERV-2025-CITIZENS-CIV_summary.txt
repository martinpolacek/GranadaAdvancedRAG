**Call for Proposals: CERV-2025-CITIZENS-CIV — Citizens’ engagement and participation**

**1. WHO:**

*   **Organiser:** European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA), managing the call under the Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values Programme (CERV)
*   **Eligible Applicants:**
    *   Lead Applicants (Coordinator): Non-profit private legal entities or public universities
    *   Co-applicants: Non-profit legal entities (public or private bodies) or international organizations
    *   All applicants (beneficiaries and affiliated entities) must be legal entities established in:
        *   EU Member States (including overseas countries and territories (OCTs)).
        *   Countries associated to the CERV Programme or countries which are in ongoing negotiations for an association agreement and where the agreement enters into force before grant signature.
    * Other entities may participate in other consortium roles, such as associated partners or subcontractors.

**2. WHAT:**

*   **Type of Program:** EU Action Grant under the Citizens, Equality, Rights and Values (CERV) Programme.
*   **Focus of Project:**  Projects must foster citizens' engagement and participation in the democratic and civic life of the Union by making known and publicly exchanging their views in all areas of Union action. There is a focus on three priorities:
    1.  Promoting exchanges on future Union policy priorities and challenges
    2.  Countering disinformation, information manipulation and interference in the democratic debate
    3.  Promoting citizens’ active engagement and democratic participation

**3. HOW:**

*   **Submission Procedure:**
    *   Proposals must be submitted electronically via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System. Paper submissions are not accepted.
    *   A user account and organisation registration (PIC) are required for submission.
*   **Application Package**
     *   Application Form Part A (administrative info and summarized budget - online)
     *   Application Form Part B (technical description - download, complete, and re-upload as PDF; max 70 pages)
    *   Part C (KPI) - contains additional project data and the project’s contribution to EU programme key performance indicators (to be filled in directly online)
     *   Lump-Sum Budget Calculator (download, complete, and re-upload).
     *   List of previous projects (template provided in Part B).
     *   Child protection policy covering the four areas described in the Keeping Children Safe Child Safeguarding Standards for private organisations. Public entities must submit a declaration on honour on the respect of child protection requirements at GAP stage.
*   **Key Requirements for Participants:**
    *   Legal entities (public or private bodies) established in eligible countries
    *   Minimum of 2 applicants from 2 different eligible countries (lead applicant + at least one co-applicant)
    *   Adherence to ethical standards and EU values
    *   The EU grant applied for cannot be lower than EUR 75 000
    *   Project must be transnational

**4. WHY:**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   To promote citizens' and representative associations’ participation in and contribution to the democratic and civic life of the Union
    *   To foster a deeper understanding of the EU's priorities and democratic challenges
    *   To empower citizens to take an active role in representative democracy, including electoral participation.
    *   To enhance the capacity of citizens and media to critically evaluate online information and engage in a safer digital environment
*   **Expected Results:**
    *   Increased citizens' awareness of rights and EU values.
    *   Enhanced opportunities for citizens to express their views and make them heard.
    *   Improved knowledge and understanding of EU institutions and policies.
    *   Increased citizen empowerment to engage in decision-making from local to EU levels.
    *   Strengthened democratic participation, especially among younger and older people, women, mobile EU citizens, and people with disabilities.
    *   Increased situational awareness, resilience and preparedness to counter disinformation and information manipulation

**5. HOW MUCH:**

*   **Amount of Support:** The estimated available call budget is EUR 33,000,000. Specific budget allocation per priority is detailed in the call document.
*   **Financial Scope of Projects:**
    *   Minimum grant amount: EUR 75,000
    *   Maximum grant amount: No limit
*   **Co-financing Rate:** 100%
*   **Type of Grant:** Lump sum grant
