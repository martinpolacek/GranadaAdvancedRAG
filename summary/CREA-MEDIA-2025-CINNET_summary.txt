**Creative Europe Programme (CREA) — Call: CREA-MEDIA-2025-CINNET – Networks of European Cinemas**

*   **WHO:**
    *   **Organiser:** European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA) under the powers delegated by the European Commission.
    *   **Eligible Applicants:** Legal entities (public or private bodies) established in Creative Europe Participating Countries (EU Member States and listed EEA countries and countries associated to the Creative Europe Programme). Cinema network must represent at least 400 cinemas in at least 20 MEDIA strand participating countries. A European independent cinema is a company, association or organisation with a single screen, or several screens based in countries participating in the MEDIA strand. The screening of films should be the principal activity of the participant or division of the participant's organisation. The participating cinemas must be registered in one of the countries participating in the MEDIA strand.

*   **WHAT:**
    *   **Type:** Call for proposals under the MEDIA strand of the Creative Europe Programme.
    *   **Focus:** Supporting networks of European cinemas to encourage the screening of non-national European films, foster audience engagement (especially among young people), promote innovative approaches, and encourage collaboration and knowledge sharing within the cinema industry.

*   **HOW:**
    *   **Application Procedure:**
        *   Submit proposals electronically via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System before the deadline. Paper submissions are not accepted.
        *   The proposal must include administrative forms (Part A), a technical description (Part B, max 70 pages), additional project data (Part C), and required annexes.
        *   Registration in the Participant Register is mandatory for beneficiaries and affiliated entities.
    *   **Key Requirements:**
        *   Applicants must represent a network of at least 400 cinemas in at least 20 MEDIA participating countries.
        *   Activities must align with the objectives of the MEDIA strand and address the specified themes and priorities.

*   **WHY:**
    *   **Objectives:**
        *   Enhance the circulation, promotion, and distribution of European audiovisual works.
        *   Promote European works and engage audiences, particularly young audiences.
        *   Reinforce and renew the cinema-going experience.
        *   Foster innovation and collaboration within the European cinema sector.
    *   **Expected Results:**
        *   Increased audience for non-national European films.
        *   Wider and more diverse audiences, including young people.
        *   More competitive and innovative cinema theatres.
        *   Improved collaboration among cinema operators.

*   **HOW MUCH:**
    *   **Budget:** EUR 15,500,000
    *   **Support:** Supports up to 95% of eligible costs.
    *   **Financial Support to Third Parties (FSTP):** Allowed, up to EUR 250,000 annually per third party; amounts of more than EUR 60,000 per third party are necessary because the nature of the actions under this call is such that their objectives would otherwise be impossible or overly difficult to achieve.

*   **OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION:**
    *   **Deadline:** 16 July 2025, 17:00:00 CET (Brussels)
    *   **Project Duration:** Projects should normally not exceed 21 months.
    *   **Eligible Activities:** Networking, financial support to cinemas, promotion of European films, innovative activities for young audiences, digital transition activities, sustainable business practices, and collaboration with other platforms.
    *   **Ethics:** Projects must comply with ethical standards and applicable laws.
    *   **Key Documents:** Call Document, Model Grant Agreement, EU Funding & Tenders Portal Online Manual, EU Grants AGA — Annotated Grant Agreement.
    *    **Open Access:** Open access to peer-reviewed scientific publications relating to their results at the latest at the time of publication through a trusted repository; publication fees in full open access venues are eligible for reimbursement.
    *   **Data protection:** The beneficiaries must process personal data under the Agreement in compliance with the applicable EU, international and national law on data protection (in particular, Regulation 2016/679).
    *   **Specific Logos:** Projects should display the European flag (emblem) and funding statement and the Creative Europe Media logo, and animated logo for certain actions.
    *   **Reporting Language:** Reporting must be in the language of the Agreement, unless otherwise agreed with the granting authority.
    *   **Additional Notes:** The information provided is subject to the provisions and guidelines set out in the Creative Europe Programme regulations and related documents. The call is subject to the final adoption of the budget by the EU budgetary authority.
