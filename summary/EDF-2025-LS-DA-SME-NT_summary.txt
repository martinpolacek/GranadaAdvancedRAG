**SUMMARY OF EUROPEAN DEFENCE FUND APPLICATION AND RELATED DOCUMENTS**

**1. WHO:**

*   **Organiser:** European Commission, Directorate-General for Defence Industry and Space (DG DEFIS). In some cases, projects may be managed by the European Defence Agency (EDA) after selection.
*   **Eligible Applicants:** Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) are the target applicants for the call EDF-2025-LS-DA-SME-NT.
    *   Must be legal entities (public or private bodies) established in eligible countries, have their executive management structure in eligible countries, and not be subject to control by a non-associated third country or entity.
    *   Must be registered and validated SMEs in the Participant Register.

**2. WHAT:**

*   **Type of Program/Call:** European Defence Fund (EDF) action grant. Specifically for lump sum grants for development actions dedicated to SMEs.
*   **Focus:** Innovative defence products, solutions, and technologies, including those adapting civil technologies or addressing hybrid warfare. Open to any area of interest for defence.

**3. HOW:**

*   **Application Procedure:** Submit proposals electronically via the EU Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System. Paper submissions are not accepted.
*   **Key Requirements for Participants:**
    *   Register in the Participant Register and obtain a PIC.
    *   Complete and upload Application Form Part A (administrative information).
    *   Complete and upload Application Form Part B (technical description), along with mandatory annexes in a password-protected zip archive.
    *   Provide a password for the zip archive via email to DEFIS-EDF-PROPOSALS-PWD@ec.europa.eu before the submission deadline.
*   **Format Requirements:** Specific templates must be used for Part B and annexes. Proposals have a page limit (typically 100 pages for Part B). Minimum font size Arial 9, A4 page size, and specified margins. Exceeding the page limit will result in the extra pages being disregarded.

**4. WHY:**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   Foster the competitiveness, efficiency, and innovation capacity of the European defence technological and industrial base (EDTIB).
    *   Contribute to EU strategic autonomy and freedom of action.
    *   Encourage cross-border cooperation between legal entities, particularly SMEs.
    *   Promote better exploitation of innovation, research, and technological development in the defence sector.
*   **Expected Results:**
    *   Innovative, rapid, and cost-effective defence solutions.
    *   Ground-breaking technologies and approaches in the defence sector.
    *   Enhanced innovation capacity among SMEs in Europe.
    *   Future market creation for SMEs, especially in defence markets.
    *   Strengthened EDTIB ecosystems and EU/Associated Country defence supply chains.

**5. HOW MUCH:**

*   **Amount of Support:**  Budget of EUR 33,000,000 is expected. Lump sum grants will be awarded. The maximum lump sum grant amount per project may not exceed EUR 6,000,000.
*   **Co-financing Rate:** The funding rate varies depending on the activity type and bonuses for SME/mid-cap participation and PESCO involvement.  Baseline funding rates can be up to 90% for studies, 65% for integrating knowledge and increasing efficiency, and 20-70% for other types of activities. Bonuses of up to 10% are available for PESCO projects and 5-10% for SME/Mid-Cap participation. The sum of PESCO and SME/Mid-cap bonus will never exceed 100%.

**6. OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION:**

*   **Deadline for Submission:** 16 October 2025, 17:00:00 CET (Brussels).
*   **Specific Conditions/Limitations:**
    *   Mandatory multi-beneficiary applications (minimum 3 independent applicants from 3 different eligible countries).
    *   The partners must be SMEs.
    *   Subcontracting limited to 30% of total eligible costs per beneficiary/affiliated entity.
    *   Geographic restrictions: Activities must take place in eligible countries.
    *   Projects must meet specific criteria related to defence capability requirements and technical specifications.
    *   Projects must be non-duplicative and in line with EU policy interests.
*   **Essential Details:**
    *   Complete application (Parts A and B, mandatory annexes), adherence to page limits, use of correct templates, and timely submission are critical.
    *   Ethics and security self-assessments are required. Projects must comply with ethical principles and security regulations.
    *   Consortium agreement is recommended.
    *   Applicants are to read all the related documents carefully, such as Model Grant Agreement, rules for Legal Entity Validation, and FAQ.
    *   Subcontractors and associated partners must comply with the same eligibility as beneficiaries.
