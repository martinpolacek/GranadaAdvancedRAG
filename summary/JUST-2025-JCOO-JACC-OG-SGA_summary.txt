**Justice Programme (JUST) - Operating Grants**

1.  **WHO:**
    *   **Organiser:** The European Commission, Directorate-General for Justice and Consumers (DG JUST) manages the call.
    *   **Eligible Applicants:** Single applicants (networks) that are non-profit, private bodies, and established formal European-level networks. They must have a Framework Partnership Agreement (FPA) under Call JUST-2025-JCOO-JACC-OG-FPA and member organizations from at least 14 EU Member States or associated countries. Activities must align with the objectives of the Justice Programme.

2.  **WHAT:**
    *   **Type:** Operating Grant Call for Proposals.
    *   **Focus:** To support the 2026 work programme of European networks active in facilitating and promoting judicial cooperation in civil and/or criminal matters, and/or in the area of access to justice.

3.  **HOW:**
    *   **Application Procedure:** Submit proposals electronically via the EU Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System. Paper submissions are not accepted. The submission process involves two steps: 1) creating a user account and registering the organization, and 2) submitting the proposal.
    *   **Key Requirements for Participants:** Complete the Application Form (Parts A and B), KPI Tool, provide CVs of core project team, Activity report of the last year, list of previous projects and a child protection policy (if applicable), plus a list of network members. Legal entities (beneficiaries and affiliated entities) must register in the Participant Register and be validated.

4.  **WHY:**
    *   **Objectives:**
        *   In the area of judicial cooperation: facilitate and support judicial cooperation in civil and criminal matters, promote the rule of law, independence and impartiality of the judiciary.
        *   In the area of access to justice: facilitate effective and non-discriminatory access to justice, promote efficient procedures, and support the rights of victims and suspects.
    *   **Expected Results:** Increased capacity of national practitioners, authorities, and justice systems; Strengthened cooperation and information exchange; Improved efficiency, resilience, and knowledge; Harmonization of practices; Acceleration of proceedings; Improved situation for individuals subject to measures, and Enhanced public awareness.

5.  **HOW MUCH:**
    *   **Amount of Support:**  The estimated available call budget is EUR 2,035,000 for promoting judicial cooperation in civil and/or criminal matters and EUR 2,390,000 for access to justice. The grant awarded may be lower than the amount requested.
    *   **Financial Scope of Projects:** Project budgets (requested grant amount) are expected to be in line with the multiannual budget estimate as indicated in the framework partnership agreement proposal.
    *   **Co-financing Rate:** The funding rate is 80% of the eligible costs.

        *   Proposals are limited to a maximum of 45 pages (Part B).
