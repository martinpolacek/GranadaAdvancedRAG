**Union Civil Protection Mechanism (UCPM) - Technical Assistance for Disaster Risk Management (Track 1) - Call for Proposals**

**1. WHO:**

*   **Organiser:** European Commission, Directorate-General for European Civil Protection and Humanitarian Aid Operations (DG ECHO).
*   **Eligible Applicants (Beneficiaries and Affiliated Entities):**
    *   Legal entities (public bodies).
    *   National authorities.
    *   Established in:
        *   EU Member States (including overseas countries and territories (OCTs))
        *   UCPM Participating States: Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Iceland, the Republic of Moldova, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Norway, Serbia, Türkiye, Ukraine
        *   Other countries: Georgia and Kosovo.

**2. WHAT:**

*   **Type:** Call for proposals for EU action grants.
*   **Focus:** Technical Assistance for Disaster Risk Management (Track 1). This involves providing financial support to eligible countries to develop strategic disaster risk management actions and to enhance institutional and technical capacity for disaster risk management, climate adaptation and resilience building.

**3. HOW:**

*   **Application Procedure:**
    *   Electronic submission only via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System.
    *   Applications must include:
        *   Application Form Part A (administrative data, completed online).
        *   Application Form Part B (technical description, uploaded as PDF using the provided template, maximum 60 pages).
        *   Detailed budget table (using provided template).
        *   Letter of Support from the competent national civil protection authority (except for applicants which themselves are the national civil protection authority) (specific Word template available in the Submission System).
        *   Annex of key performance indicators (KPIs) available in the UCP Knowledge Network platform.
    *   Applicants must confirm the mandate to act for all participants, correctness of information, and compliance with eligibility criteria.
*   **Key Requirements for Participants:**
    *   Legal entities (public bodies).
    *   National authorities with DRM responsibilities.
    *   Established in eligible countries.
    *   Register in the Participant Register and be validated by the Central Validation Service.

**4. WHY:**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   Support Member States’ actions in implementing the Union disaster resilience goals.
    *   Enhance institutional and technical capacity for preparing, implementing, monitoring, evaluating, and improving strategic disaster risk management activities.
    *   Sustain efforts to anticipate future systemic shocks by fostering a culture of prevention.
    *   Support the preparation and implementation of prevention and preparedness investments, strategies, and reforms.
    *   Further develop plans, procedures and/or arrangements aimed at ensuring effective cross-sectoral and cross-border crisis prevention, preparedness and response.
    *   Integrate climate adaptation and resilience in disaster risk management.
    *   Support the greening of civil protection and disaster risk management authorities.
*   **Expected Results/Outcomes:**
    *   Strengthened national disaster risk assessment capabilities and management frameworks.
    *   Improved data and analysis for developing DRM policies and plans.
    *   Increased risk awareness and preparedness of the population.
    *   Improved multi-hazard disaster loss data.
    *   Progress made in the implementation of the Disaster Resilience Goals.
    *   Increased quality of disaster risk management investment plans and projects.
    *   Improved disaster risk management budgeting.
    *   More investments in disaster risk management.
    *   Increased synergies with other funding instruments.
    *   Increased cross-sectoral crisis management capabilities.
    *   Strengthened early warning systems and public warning.
    *   Better forecasting and scenario-building capacities.
    *   Integration of early warning systems in decision making.

**5. HOW MUCH:**

*   **Available Budget:** EUR 6,000,000 (estimated).
*   **Financial Support:** Action grants; mixed actual cost.
*   **Funding Rate:** 95% of eligible costs.
*   **Maximum Grant Amount:** EUR 750,000 per project.

**6. OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION:**

*   **Deadline:** 23 April 2025, 17:00:00 CET (Brussels time).
*   **Limitations:**
    *   Projects must comply with EU policy interests and priorities (environment, social, security, industrial and trade policy, EU values).
    *   The purchase of equipment and works are not eligible activities.
    *   Financial support to third parties is not allowed.
    *   Proposals requesting more than EUR 750 000 as the EU contribution are not admissible.
    *   Projects must relate to activities taking place in eligible countries.
*   **Important Points:**
    *   Early submission is strongly recommended.
    *   All participants must register in the Participant Register.
    *   The coordinator is the central contact point.
    *   A consortium agreement is recommended, if a consortium is established.
    *   The Grant Agreement comes into force on the day of signature by the granting authority or the coordinator (depending on which is later).
