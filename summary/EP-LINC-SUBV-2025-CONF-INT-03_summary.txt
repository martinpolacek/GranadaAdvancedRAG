**1. WHO: Organization and Eligible Applicants/Target Groups**

*   **Organizer:** The Directorate-General for Logistics and Interpretation for Conferences (DG LINC) of the European Parliament is organizing the financial support program.
*   **Eligible Applicants:**
    *   Higher Education Institutions, Universities, university institutes, consortia, and associations of universities or institutes offering or coordinating postgraduate courses specializing in conference interpreting.
    *   Associations, consortia, and bodies whose main mission is to support cooperation and disseminate best practices in the field of worldwide conference interpreter training.
    *   For Topic 3 only: Higher-Education Institutions, Universities, university institutes, consortia and associations of universities or institutes - which offer or coordinate master, postgraduate courses or bachelor courses with conference interpreting components - registered in Ukraine.
*   The teaching establishments must be approved by their national authorities as public or private bodies charged with a public task of teaching and organizing courses at the Master's or postgraduate level in the field of conference interpreting. If the applicant is not a teaching establishment, its mission must nevertheless be related to the objectives of this program and be recognized by the national authorities where it is established.
*  Applicants must be legally established in one of the Member States of the European Union, in one of the EFTA countries or in a candidate country for future membership of the European Union.

**2. WHAT: Type of Program and Focus**

*   **Type:** The program is a call for proposals for financial support for training in conference interpreting. It's a grant program.
*   **Focus:** The program aims to improve the professional quality of interpreter training, strengthen training programs, and produce new generations of qualified conference interpreters, in particular for the European Parliament and other EU institutions. Specific topics include:
    *   Promoting quality and linguistic diversity in teaching conference interpreting.
    *   Establishing postgraduate centers of excellence.
    *   Encouraging cooperation among postgraduate courses in different countries.
    *   Supporting regional cooperation among universities.
    *   Integrating modern information and communication technologies into interpreter training.
    * Organization of courses by Ukrainian Universities.

**3. HOW: Application Procedure and Key Requirements**

*   **Submission:** Applications must be submitted electronically via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System.
*   **Requirements:**
    *   Applications may be submitted in any official EU language, but English or French is strongly advised.
    *   The application form consists of Part A (online) and Part B (uploaded as PDF).
    *   Applications must include all mandatory annexes and supporting documents.
    *   Applicants must meet eligibility and selection criteria (financial and operational capacity).
    *   Teaching establishments must be approved by their national authorities.
    * Higher-Education Institutions registered in Ukraine for Topic 3
*   **Grant Selection Criteria:** Applicants must have stable and adequate financial resources to maintain their activities during the implementation of the project and must have the professional skills and qualifications to provide specialised training at the required professional level.
*   **Grant Award Criteria:** Quality, innovativeness and usability of the proposed project; potential impact on the area of conference interpreting that can be achieved; contribution to the objectives and expected results of the grant program; quality and efficiency of project management.

**4. WHY: Objectives and Expected Results**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   To ensure a sufficient number of qualified conference interpreters are available to enable the EU institutions and bodies to function properly in a context of linguistic diversity.
    *   To assist universities in training high-quality conference interpreters within priority language combinations that match the European Parliament's needs.
    *   To promote succession planning and maintain the necessary level of language coverage.
*   **Expected Results:**
    *   Development of and support for centers of excellence in conference interpreting training.
    *   Training of conference interpreters to meet current and future language needs of the EU institutions, especially the European Parliament.
    *   Improved language coverage in accordance with the needs of the European Parliament.
    *   Graduates from supported programs who participate in accreditation tests and succeed.

**5. HOW MUCH: Financial Support**

*   **Indicative Budget:** The indicative budget for the 2025 program is EUR 734,000.
*   **Support Level:** The European Parliament co-financing may not exceed 75% of the total accepted eligible costs.
*   Indirect costs are eligible for flat-rate funding of maximum 7% of the total direct costs eligible.
*   The financial support is offered as grants to universities and similar bodies.

**6. OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION**

*   **Deadlines:**
    *   Application Submission: May 7, 2025
    *   The action should end no later than July 31, 2026.
*   **Specific Conditions/Limitations:**
    *   Multiple applications and grants relating to the same topics or work programs are prohibited. The same costs cannot be financed twice by the EU budget.
    *   Costs incurred before the submission date of the application are not eligible for co-funding.
    *   Budget transfers between chapters are limited to 10% of the amount of each chapter of estimated eligible costs.
    *   During the execution of the grant agreement, the Directorate-General for Logistics and Interpretation for Conferences of the European Parliament actively monitors the action. The beneficiaries shall allow representatives of the European Parliament to participate in examination panels and meetings related to this action.
