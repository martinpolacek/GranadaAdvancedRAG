Here is a comprehensive summary of the Erasmus+ Programme's Erasmus Charter for Higher Education (ECHE) certification process, based on the provided documents:

**1. WHO:**

*   **Organizers:** The European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA) manages the call for ECHE certifications. The Erasmus+ National Agencies also play a key role in monitoring compliance with ECHE principles.
*   **Eligible Applicants:** Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) established in Erasmus+ Programme Countries (EU Member States, EEA countries, countries associated to the Erasmus+ Programme, or countries in ongoing negotiations for association) and Western Balkans third countries not associated to the Programme (Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kosovo, and Montenegro).
     *   Applicants must be recognized as HEIs by their national authorities.

**2. WHAT:**

*   **Type of Program/Call:** This is a call for Erasmus Charter for Higher Education (ECHE) quality certifications.
*   **Focus:** The ECHE is a quality certificate that sets out fundamental principles and minimum requirements for HEIs participating in the Erasmus+ Programme. It aims to reinforce the quality of student/staff mobility and transnational cooperation projects and ensures that HEIs' participation in Erasmus+ is part of their modernization and internationalization strategy.

**3. HOW:**

*   **Application Procedure:**
    *   Applications must be submitted electronically via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System.
    *   The application consists of two parts:
        *   **Part A:** Administrative information (filled in directly online).
        *   **Part B:** Technical description (Erasmus Policy Statement (EPS), HEI profile, organization and management structure, implementation of ECHE principles). This part is downloaded from the Portal Submission System, completed, and re-uploaded as a PDF.
    *   The application must be complete, containing all requested information, annexes, and supporting documents.
*   **Key Requirements for Participants:**
    *   Adherence to the ECHE principles (non-discrimination, transparency, inclusion, etc.).
    *   Commitment to automatic recognition of credits for learning outcomes achieved during mobility periods.
    *   Implementation of the European Student Card Initiative.
    *   Promotion of the Erasmus+ mobile App.
    *   Promotion of environmentally friendly practices.
    *   Promotion of civic engagement and active citizenship.

**4. WHY:**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   To ensure the quality of HEIs' participation in the Erasmus+ Programme.
    *   To reinforce the quality of student and staff mobility.
    *   To reinforce the quality of cooperation projects.
    *   To ensure HEIs' participation is part of a strategy for modernization and internationalization.
*   **Expected Results:**
    *   Award of the ECHE certificate to eligible HEIs that meet the quality standards.
    *   Improved quality and impact of Erasmus+ activities.
    *   Contribution to the creation of a European Education Area.

**5. HOW MUCH:**

*   **Amount of Support/Financial Scope:** The ECHE is a quality certification and does not automatically imply direct funding under the Erasmus+ Programme.
*   **Co-financing Rate:** Not applicable, as this is a certification process and not a grant.

**6. OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION:**

*   **Deadlines:** The call remains open for the entire 2021-2027 programming period, with yearly cut-off dates for submission, typically in January (see document section 2).
*   **Page Limit:** Part B of the application is limited to a maximum of 25 pages. Additional pages will not be considered.
*   **Language:** Applications can be submitted in any official EU language, but English is strongly advised.
*   **Monitoring:** ECHE holders will be monitored by the Erasmus+ National Agencies for compliance with ECHE principles. Failure to comply may lead to withdrawal of the certificate.
*   **Withdrawal:** The EACEA may withdraw the certificate if the institution loses its status as a higher education institution or no longer complies with ECHE quality standards.
* **Call identifier**: ERASMUS-EDU-2022-ECHE-CERT
