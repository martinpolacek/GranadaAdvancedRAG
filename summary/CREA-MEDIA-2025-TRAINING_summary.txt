## Creative Europe Programme (CREA) - MEDIA Strand: Skills and Talent Development Call Summary

Here's a comprehensive summary of the provided data, structured according to the requested key information:

**1. WHO:**

*   **Organiser:** European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA), managing the call on behalf of the European Commission.
*   **Eligible Applicants:** Legal entities (public or private bodies) established in Creative Europe Participating Countries, including:
    *   EU Member States (including overseas countries and territories (OCTs))
    *   Non-EU countries: listed EEA countries and countries associated to the Creative Europe Programme or in ongoing negotiations.
  *   Single and consortium applications are allowed
  *   EU bodies (with the exception of the European Commission Joint Research Centre) are **NOT** eligible
  *   Natural persons are **NOT** eligible (with the exception of self-employed persons, i.e. sole traders, where the company does not have legal personality separate from that of the natural person.

**2. WHAT:**

*   **Type of Program:** EU Action Grant under the Creative Europe Programme (MEDIA Strand).
*   **Focus:**  **Skills and Talent Development** Specifically, training and mentoring activities designed to enhance the capacity of audiovisual professionals to adapt to new creative processes, market developments, and digital technologies affecting the entire value chain. Focus is also put on new creative processes, harnessing digital innovation, uptake of digital tools for videogames production and distribution; development of new business models and enhancing IP rights exploitation; green transition.

**3. HOW:**

*   **Application Procedure:**
    *   Submit electronically via the Funding & Tenders Portal Electronic Submission System. Paper submissions are not allowed.
    *   Two-step process:
        *   Create a user account and register the organization in the Participant Register (get a PIC).
        *   Submit the proposal via the Electronic Submission System.
*   **Key Requirements for Participants:**
    *   Adhere to formatting guidelines (page limit, font size, margins).
    *   Submit all required documents via the Portal Submission System.
    *   Ensure activities comply with EU policy interests and priorities.
    *   Demonstrate technical and financial resources to carry out the project.
    *   Comply with ethical standards and applicable EU, international and national law.
    *   Ensure impartiality and editorial independence during project implementation (if applicable).
    *   Provide explicit consent of all applicants on their participation and on the content of the proposal.
    *   Ensure that all ethics issues are taken into account and that, if any ethics issues apply, the ethics self-assessment is completed.
    *   Ensure a good representativeness of gender and diversity both in project organizers and participants.
    *    Ensure that at least 20% of the total number of participants must be offered reduced fees.
    *    Respect data protection regulations

**4. WHY:**

*   **Objectives:**
    *   Enhance the capacity of audiovisual professionals to adapt to new creative processes, market developments, and digital technologies.
    *   Promote sustainable and environmentally respectful solutions for the audiovisual industry.
    *    Foster growth and investment through greater exploitation of IP across the EU and beyond.
    *   Equip audiovisual and gaming professionals with a new combination of creative and digital skills, thereby increasing the competitiveness potential of the European industry.
*   **Expected Results:**
    *   Sustainable and more environmentally respectful solutions for the audiovisual industry.
    *   European creative talent harnessed by embracing new creative processes.
    *   Digital transition of the audiovisual sector supported for content creation and dissemination.
    *   Growth and investment fostered through greater IP exploitation.
    *   Audiovisual and gaming professionals equipped with a new combination of creative and digital skills.

**5. HOW MUCH:**

*   **Total Call Budget:** EUR 7,500,000
*   **Financial Scope:** No limit
*   **Funding Rate:** 80% of eligible costs
*   **Form of Grant:** Budget-based (actual costs) with elements of unit costs and flat-rate funding.
*   **No Financial support to third parties**

**6. OTHER IMPORTANT INFORMATION:**

*   **Deadline:** 24 April 2025, 17:00:00 CET (Brussels time)
*   **Project Duration:** Normally should not exceed 12 months.
*   **Eligible Costs:** Personnel, subcontracting, travel & subsistence (using unit costs), equipment (depreciation), and other direct costs.  Indirect costs covered by a flat rate of 7% of eligible direct costs (excluding volunteers and certain specific costs).
*   **Limitations:** Financial support to third parties is not allowed. Applications are limited to a maximum of 70 pages (Part B). Ensure compliance with SME definitions if claiming SME status. Pre-financing guarantees may be required depending on financial capacity.
*   **Specific Conditions:** Ensure best value for money in procurement and avoid conflicts of interest. Proposals should promote sustainable and inclusive activities. Beneficiaries must commit to and ensure the respect of basic EU values
*   **Ineligible Costs:** Costs related to return on capital, debt service, provisions, interest owed, currency exchange losses, deductible VAT, costs incurred during suspension, and in-kind contributions by third parties.

This summary encapsulates the key information from the provided documents in a structured and concise manner. Be sure to always refer to the official call documents for complete and up-to-date details.
